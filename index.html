About me

A short version of a long story

I've been fascinated by timing since a PhD rotation project where a 200-millisecond delay in a behavioral task turned out to explain a decade-old puzzle in the literature. Since then my work has sat at the intersection of computational modeling, systems neuroscience, and clinical collaboration — trying to understand time not as a passive backdrop for cognition, but as something the brain actively constructs.

Before joining Riverbank University, I completed a postdoc at Alderidge Institute working on recurrent network models of interval timing, and a PhD in Neuroscience where I combined EEG and behavioral psychophysics to study duration perception. Outside the lab, I mentor first-generation PhD applicants and spend an unreasonable amount of time thinking about long-distance trail running.

My lab is always interested in hearing from prospective students and collaborators — see the CV page for a full academic history, or the research page for what we're currently working on.
